About prosthetic legs

What they are and how they work

A prosthetic leg is a device used to support users who are missing all or part of their limb due to an injury, accident, illness, or congenital condition.

Prosthetic legs can empower people with limb differences in many different ways. They can help users regain their mobility and independence, pursue their favourite hobbies and pastimes, get back to their jobs, and improve their overall quality of life.

These devices come in a variety of designs and formats that meet different user needs and support different activities and goals. With the right prosthetic leg, users can live fulfilling lives and pursue their most important goals.

Restoring freedom of movement
Components

Key parts of a prosthetic leg

Prosthetic legs can come in many configurations depending on the user's lifestyle, interests, and residual limb. Typically a prosthetic leg has up to three main components.

Component 01

Socket & Liner

The socket together with the liner and corresponding suspension mode are the most individual part of every prosthetic leg. The liner is worn directly onto the limb to protect it, and ensures a safe, secure connection to the socket, which bears the body weight and connects to prosthetic components.

Component 02

Prosthetic Knee

For users with an above-knee limb difference, this component provides much of the prosthetic leg's mobility and stability. It replaces the natural knee joint and is designed to enable a wide range of motion — including walking, climbing stairs, and navigating ramps. Some prosthetic knees can even be taken into water.

Component 03

Prosthetic Foot

This component is primarily designed to provide a stable, supportive walking experience. Prosthetic feet come in a range of different designs and configurations with varying levels of stiffness, flexibility, and responsiveness. A professional prosthetist will help find the foot that offers the right performance for your lifestyle.

Types of prosthetic legs

Two main kinds of prosthetic legs

There are two main types of prosthetic legs: mechanical prostheses and microprocessor-controlled devices. Both prosthetic feet and knees are available in both forms, providing a range of options to suit different users and their needs.

⚙️

Mechanical Prostheses

These devices use hydraulic and other mechanisms to provide the support and stability users need to stand, walk, and perform daily activities. They are typically strong, durable devices that can withstand significant wear and tear — a reliable and valuable option for many users.

🔬

Microprocessor-Controlled

Highly advanced devices that use computerized systems, sensors, and hydraulics to mimic natural walking gait. They offer a more sophisticated level of control and customization, adapting to different terrains and activities — offering greater stability, balance, and a more natural feel.

How it works

The fitting process

Getting a prosthetic leg is a collaborative journey. No two users need the same solution — every prosthetic leg is customised for the person who will be using it. Here is what to expect.

1

Initial Consultation

Meet with our certified prosthetist to discuss your limb difference, lifestyle goals, and activity level. We listen and assess your needs thoroughly.

2

Component Selection

Together with your care team — including your doctor and physical therapist — we identify the right set of prosthetic leg components for your life.

3

Custom Socket Build

Our prosthetist custom-builds a socket that fits your residual limb perfectly, keeping you comfortable, safe, and active throughout the day.

4

Training & Follow-Up

We guide you through the training process and provide ongoing follow-up care to ensure your prosthetic leg continues to serve you well.
